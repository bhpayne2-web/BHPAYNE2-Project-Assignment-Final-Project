Daily Itinerary

Day 1: Magic Kingdom Classics

Start the trip with the most classic Disney park experience. Take castle photos, ride a few iconic attractions, and end the night with fireworks. This is a great day for couples who want that traditional Disney feeling.

Ride options: Space Mountain for thrill seekers, Pirates of the Caribbean for classic Disney fans, Haunted Mansion for spooky-attraction lovers, and Peter Pan’s Flight for a slower nostalgic ride.

Food spots: Try Be Our Guest Restaurant for a themed sit-down meal, Jungle Navigation Co. LTD Skipper Canteen for a more adventurous menu, or Sleepy Hollow for a quicker snack stop.

Day 2: EPCOT Food and World Showcase

Spend the day exploring EPCOT at a slower pace. This is a great park for couples because there are plenty of places to walk, eat, take photos, and explore different country pavilions.

Ride options: Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind for thrill seekers, Soarin’ for scenic ride fans, Frozen Ever After for Disney movie fans, and Living with the Land for a relaxed break.

Food spots: Try Chefs de France for a sit-down meal, Crêpes À Emporter by La Crêperie de Paris for a quick sweet option, or explore festival food booths if an EPCOT festival is happening.

Day 3: Hollywood Studios

Hollywood Studios is best for couples who love immersive lands, thrill rides, and movie-themed attractions. This is a good day to plan ahead because popular rides can have longer wait times.

Ride options: Star Wars: Rise of the Resistance for immersive storytelling fans, The Twilight Zone Tower of Terror for thrill seekers, Mickey & Minnie’s Runaway Railway for classic character fans, and Toy Story Mania for a fun competitive ride.

Food spots: Try Sci-Fi Dine-In Theater Restaurant for a fun themed date-night meal, Docking Bay 7 Food and Cargo for Star Wars fans, or Woody’s Lunch Box for a casual quick-service option.

Day 4: Resort Morning and Favorite Park

Use the final morning for breakfast, shopping, or relaxing at the resort. In the afternoon, return to your favorite park for final rides, photos, snacks, and souvenirs before leaving.

Best for thrill lovers: Revisit the biggest rides from the trip.
Best for food lovers: Spend extra time in EPCOT or Disney Springs.
Best for relaxed travelers: Sleep in, enjoy the resort, and keep the last day flexible.

Trip Tips

  • Book dining reservations early for popular sit-down restaurants.
  • Plan rest breaks because Disney park days can be long.
  • Check park hours before finalizing the schedule.
  • Choose rides based on your travel style: thrill rides, slow rides, shows, food, or photo spots.
